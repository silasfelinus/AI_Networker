Media 2024

Since it’s now 2025, I can now share my 2024 media list!

Since 2014 (with the exception of 2023), I’ve tracked all movies, tv series, comic books, video games, and podcasts I’ve completed through the year. 

I only count media consumed from start to finish, with a reasonable level of attention. This includes full movies, complete TV seasons, comic issues, and single player video games where I’ve reached end credits or a similar goal. 

Movies: 137
Tv (full seasons): 57
Comics (issues):  446
Video Games: 4
Books: 0-2*
Podcasts (1 podcast, 200 episodes)

Books only included the original 33 page d&d module for “Keep on the Borderlands”, and the latest D&D “Players Handbook”. I’ll admit some surprise that I read absolutely zero fiction in 2024, including audiobooks.

My singular podcast was the well-produced 200-episode horror series “The Magnus Archives”, which I finally finished by binging the last 8 episodes late yesterday evening, specifically so I could ensure its inclusion on this list.

Despite only four video games, this was a strong year for gaming, as they included Elden Ring (I finally beat the main game after sitting on the final boss for two years. I  still haven’t beaten the DLC, despite it representing a substantial amount of my 2024 playtime) and Baldur’s Gate 3 (the other two were The Corridor and Resident Evil 8 - The Village).

Some of my favorite movies in 2024 were: Saltburn, Napoleon, Poor Things, Train to Busan (an almost yearly rewatch), Late Night with the Devil, The Ministry of Ungentlemanly Warfare, RRR, Mars Express, Trap, VHS Beyond, Hellboy - The Crooked Man, Saturday Night, The Night Eats the World, Azrael, and The Substance.

Favorite TV shows included Hilda, Derry Girls, Mr and Mrs Smith, Fallout, Interview with the Vampire,  House of the Dragon, Fleabag, Better Call Saul, and Secret Level.

Favorite comics included Ex Machina (reread), East of West, She Hulk, Worldtr33, Something is Killing the Children, Fantastic Four (by Ryan North), Saga, and Alien Worlds (1992).

All in all, I’m really glad I resumed my media list. Not keeping it up in 2023 was a personal choice partly related to extended-pandemic depression. No list did change my consumption habits, I was less interested to finish a series or maintain focus, but also it meant I could watch a movie without feeling the urge to grab my phone to record the experience the moment the credits rolled.

My biggest takeaway looking at this data is the 12 month eradication of any actual book reading. I hope 2025 will see a more rounded media experience, but all-in-all, I’d say 2024 was a remarkably good year for quality media content.
