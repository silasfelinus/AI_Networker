## Concept 1 — Strange Days: A Solo Journaling RPG
**Product type:** `pdf-rpg`

A solo tabletop RPG played entirely through writing. You play as a person living in a town that's slightly *off* — nothing catastrophically wrong, just subtly strange in ways only your character notices. Each session uses a d6 table of daily events, a set of observation prompts, and a relationship web you fill in as you play. The game unfolds across 10–30 sessions of your own choosing.

**Why itch.io:** Solo journaling RPGs are itch.io's fastest-growing genre. Low barrier: no GM, no group scheduling, no special components. Price point $5–10. High potential for community pickup on solo-RPG subreddits and Itch bundles.

**Format notes:** 8–16 pages. Core rules on 1–2 pages, d6 tables (daily events, NPC quirks, strange observations), relationship map worksheet, session log template. Minimal art; evocative text layout.

**Tone:** Cozy mystery + slice-of-life + gentle surreal. Not horror. Think "Cozy Cozy Cozy" meets "Welcome to Night Vale but friendlier."

## Concept 2 — The Kindness Protocol: A Cooperative Story Game
**Product type:** `pdf-rpg`

A 1–4 player cooperative narrative game about a small community working through a challenge together: a new neighbor adjusting to a strange place, a community garden that needs saving, a local event going sideways. The central mechanic inverts the usual resource logic: *giving* things to other players is what generates story momentum. Holding onto resources slows the story down.

**Why itch.io:** Strong fit for itch.io's "cozy games" and "games for good" tags. Cross-markets with the Acts of Kindness coloring book theme. Price point $5–12. Could bundle with future coloring content as a "Kindness Pack."

**Format notes:** 10–20 pages. Rules-light: one core mechanic + three scenario seeds. Expandable with future scenario packs. Illustrated with simple character silhouettes or icons.

**Scope guard:** No rules system that requires licensed mechanics; build clean and original.

## Concept 3 — Tiny Scouts: A One-Page Family Adventure RPG
**Product type:** `pdf-rpg`

A complete tabletop RPG that fits on one page (front and back). Designed for families with kids ages 7+. Players are scouts on a nature-discovery quest: find the lost campfire gear, identify the mysterious tracks, help the injured fox. Uses a single d6 and a "try something, describe what happens" resolution. The included adventure runs 45–90 minutes.

**Why itch.io:** One-page RPGs are perennially popular and highly shareable on itch. Family-friendly games are underserved. Free or $1–3 price point builds audience fast; follow-up adventure packs can be $3–5 each.

**Format notes:** Literally one page (two sides), designed for printing and immediate play. Rules: 1/3 page. Characters: 1/3 page. Adventure: 1/3 page. Can include a separate printer-friendly cover sheet.

**Tone:** Cheerful, accessible, adventure-focused. No violence beyond "the scary situation is resolved." No reading required for players (GM reads, players act).

## Concept 4 — Fogbound: A Coastal Mystery One-Shot RPG
**Product type:** `pdf-rpg`

A 2–4 player + GM short-session tabletop RPG set in a fog-covered fishing town where something is quietly wrong. Designed to run in 2–3 hours. Uses its own simple resolution system (2d6 + trait). The mystery has three parallel threads that interweave — players will only have time for two in one session, making replay feel fresh.

**Why itch.io:** Atmospheric one-shots with their own system travel well on itch; they're self-contained and don't require an existing game purchase. "Coastal horror" and "mystery RPG" are popular itch tags. Price point $5–10.

**Format notes:** 20–30 pages. Setting overview (2–3 pages), rules (4 pages), mystery structure (10+ pages with NPC cards, location descriptions, resolution paths). Include a printer-friendly version.

**Tone:** Cozy gothic, not grimdark. Unsettling atmosphere, no gore, resolution is hopeful.

## Concept 5 — Neighborhood Watch: A System-Agnostic Mystery Module
**Product type:** `pdf-rpg`

A mystery adventure module designed to plug into any rules-light system (OSR, PbtA, Ironsworn, etc.). Set in a contemporary suburb. Something small went missing — a beloved community mural, a neighborhood cat, a longtime local shop — and the investigation reveals something heartfelt underneath. Three suspect threads, one twist, a satisfying non-combat resolution.

**Why itch.io:** System-agnostic modules reach the widest itch audience (everyone who plays any system can use it). Contemporary setting is underrepresented vs. fantasy. Price point $4–8.

**Format notes:** 12–20 pages. Location cards, NPC profiles, clue structure, resolution paths. GM-facing language only; no player-facing handouts needed. Clean two-column layout.

**Tone:** Warm community mystery, not thriller. The "villain" is a sympathetic character with understandable motives.

## Concept 6 — Color Your Campaign: A Coloring & World-Building Zine (Experiment)
**Product type:** `pdf-coloring` (experimental itch.io crossover)

A coloring zine aimed at tabletop RPG players and GMs. Each page is a setting element to color *and* annotate: a town map outline, a tavern floor plan, a village square, a dungeon entrance. Short prompts in the margins invite the colorist to name things, invent lore, and make the scene their own. Doubles as a GM prep tool and a relaxing creative activity.

**Why itch.io:** Bridges the coloring-book and TTRPG audiences — both are strong on itch. The "GM prep" angle adds utility beyond pure coloring. Would be genuinely novel on the platform. Price point $4–8.

**Format notes:** 10–15 pages. Black-and-white line art maps and scene sketches, medium complexity. Margin prompts must be very short (3–7 words). Include a blank "your notes" page.

**Scope guard:** No maps that resemble real locations. No copyrighted settings or system art.
